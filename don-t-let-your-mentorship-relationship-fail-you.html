Don't Let Your Mentorship Relationship Fail You

Most of my past mentoring relationships failed, save one. From what I've seen, most people, whether mentee or mentor, are also not successful. What do I mean by failed? I didn't ask my mentor questions, or my mentor didn't help me in a useful way (by no fault of theirs, they were busy).

The successful mentorship was partly a result of me paying my mentor. Most importantly, this changed how I acted:

  • My mentor and I only messaged in one place, in a private 1-1 slack chatroom.
  • I asked him any question I had, as many as I had
  • I messaged him at any time of day, about anything, even if I didn't have an explicit question
  • I knew what I should ask him for help with
  • I knew what my desired outcomes were for the relationship

How to Have a Successful Mentoring Relationship

So, if I were to help you as my mentee and have us be successful, here's what I'd want you to do:

  • Ask me what I can help you with
  • Pro-actively share anything that's going on, related to our work together. Email replies, questions on how to write tough emails, and so on.
  • If I don't reply, keep asking me at a reasonable interval until I reply. I wouldn't be your mentor if I didn't want to help you. Things get busy sometimes and people need reminders.
  • Tell me if something feels urgent and need advice (e.g. you want to email someone back in the next two days and want my advice before you send)
  • If I don't reply in time, you take action anyway
  • If you ask me for advice on something, then you should take my advice, or tell me why you think you can't take it. That way we make progress even if my advice feels too much for you.
  • Show me that you want my help. You can ask me anything, but obviously try not to use me as your personal google search bar.
