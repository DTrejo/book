Addendum on WTF Are VCs, Notes from a Conversation with a YC Founder

VCs

  • if they are late in their fund's cycle and you take their money you will be forced to grow faster than you want, and also exit when the fund ends. this means a successful 8-year company will be cut short at 5yrs to satisfy the VCs.
  • if you have revenue, the longer you wait the better the position you are, and the worse position the VCs are. also the more they will want to get in.
  • if you are like github and dont take VC money for a really long time, and dont need it, the VCs will start hounding you: they use their networks to have your friends at companies they've investing in invite you to dinners, and then the VC is there and ready to pitch you.
  • github finally took VC money in order to get VCs to stop bothering them
  • in this position you can ask for the craziest terms and they must accept: no firing founders, only one board seat, any valuation you want.
  • if you do have reveneue, you shouldnt even want VC money b/c it's probably not going to do much for you.
  • if you dont have revenue, you're screwed - have to suck up to vcs, spend a lot of effort on raising, get bad terms, …
