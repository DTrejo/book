How Do I Ask Friends for Referrals?

You want more referrals for your business. You aren't getting enough, but you're also not sure how to get them. Here is a detailed step-by-step way to ask all your friends for referrals, with a maximum level of personal-ness and minimum level of effort on your part. When you've set this up, it will pull your life forward—you'll wake up with referrals in your inbox, your friends will be emailing with you, you'll have created people on the lookout for your ideal client (and lying in wait, hoping to send you someone).

This is written in check-list format, to help you work through it more quickly. Try to think as little as possible and follow the steps. Thinking is what will slow you down. This might take you a week to finish, since you probably won't be working on it full time. Don't get down on yourself if it takes longer—all that matters is that you finish it.

Ingredients

  • $49/month subscription to quickmail
  • $5/month subscription to boomerang
  • free: streak.com for gmail
  • google docs
  • optional: a spare gmail account with your name in it, so that you don't have to give quickmail access to your main email account (security!).
  • your enthusiasm

Step 1: Create a List of Friends to Ask

  • [ ] look through all your gmail contacts
  • [ ] add every friend and friendly acquaintance to a gmail group called "referrals." Mindset: you can help people with your service. your friends would want their friends to get your help. your friends would want their friends to work together. your friends want to feel helpful to their friends, AND to you. you are doing a benefit to the world with your service, if someone does not want to give referrals they can spend 30 seconds to reply "not atthe moment, sorry!" you are also offering to hlep them, so it's not a "take take take" situation AT ALL. And you do want to know how they are doing.
  • [ ] enable hotkeys and use "j", "k", "x", and "l" to save your mouse hand
  • [ ] export contacts from the "referrals" group from gmail in outlook format
  • [ ] import to csv, either in excel or google docs
  • [ ] sort by emails to the top (not every one of your contacts has an email, so ignore the ones who don't)
  • [ ] add column "needs customization" - mark with x for every person who could be an ideal client and who you want to make sure you email later.
  • [ ] sort customization ones to bottom. you can handle them when you've run out of easily emailed people. don't want to get hung up on customizing emails—it's the biggest time-suck.
  • [ ] fix emails, so that you are emailing the address for the person they are most likely to answer
  • [ ] rename the column with their best email to "email"
  • [ ] gotcha: dedupe contacts, don't email the same person twice
  • [ ] make a copy of the the spreadsheet that does not contain the people who need customization
  • [ ] rename the column with their first name to "firstname"
