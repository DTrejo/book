6 questions from Anthony Cruz

How important are the cs classes and non-cs classes I take?

CS classes: important, depending on what part of industry you want to get a job in. Non-cs classes: not as important. Writing skill is what will make your life amazing in the future, definitely cultivate that, hopefully in ways that are fun for you and not grindy. e.g. consider doing 750words.com every day for a summer (I did this and loved it). Writing tips: http://dtrejo.com/how-to-write

Does it even matter which cs classes I take when applying for cs positions?

It somewhat does, to show you took challenging classes, and also if the engineering positions need certain expertise like machine learning, systems, networks, distributed systems, math, etc.

Will a low gpa due to non-cs classes negatively affect me as a university student/ new grad?

No. You can list your CS GPA on your resume. employers don't care about non-CS classes. If your overall GPA is under 3.0, you can leave it off your resume (and recruiters will know it is below 3.0). If it is above 3.0, list it. Choose to share the GPA that is higher, your overall GPA vs your CS GPA.

When I apply for an internship am I better off picking between backend or front end, or saying that I am flexible with both and can do whichever the company thinks I'm a better fit for (I am more of a flexible full stack engineer so I don't mind doing one or the other)?

Hmm that's a tough one. Choose whichever problem feels it is calling to you more, whichever feels the most painful to your co-workers or users. This is an easy way to know that your work will be appreciated. Listen for the pain, ask questions about how they handle the problem today without help from software.

As an underrepresented minority (Latino) how will I feel about the culture? Will I be able to fit in at smaller companies that probably lack a lot of diversity?

I am white-passing, so I think that it wasn't a big deal for me. I do have a Latino last name, I have no idea how that affected anything. Definitely follow my guidelines for picking companies and also consider not joining if you don't know anyone who you like at the company.

Susan on intervieing

"Also wanted to ask, in regards to advice you had on interviewing for companies you didn't care about in fall, and ones you care about in spring, don't a ton of big companies finish recruiting in the fall? Was under this impression since the spring tech career fair always seemed smaller."

The fall career fair is bigger because full-time recruiting is bigger. So, if you're looking for full time work, you'd start looking in september (or hell, if you're super ambitious, even during july/august while at your last internship as an about-to-be-senior).

The spring career fair is focused on interns, which is why it makes sense for freshman, sophomores, juniors to interview with companies they don't care about in first semester, because second semester they can get their internship. Of course, sooner is ALWAYS better, so if you feel ready, start sooner! Just make sure that when you mke the mental shift to looking at companies you actually do want to join, follow the job searching advice, so you dont waste time on companies you dont want to join (viciously stop talking to any company who doesnt fit your criteria).

How to handle rejection?

Rejection is your friend—get excited that 1) you got practice 2) you learned something about how to interview 3) you don't have to waste your valuable time following up with a company that doesn't want you anymore (and which would not enjoy working for if they didn't think you'd be a good "fit").

Ali also asks

"Hey david, i have a quick question. I always hear about people getting interviews for internships at hackathons and recruiter events. When i'm at these events, i feel so awkward to just throw my resume at these recruiters. how do you go about trying to get a job here without being rude?

No dumb questions dude, don't ever think that. There is only a question that one did not research enough fully prior to asking :) this one is a good one. Shriram said something wise about asking questions once, but I don't remember it. You should ask him what he said :)

Which language to use while interviewing?
