Why did I write this book?

I found myself giving the same advice to friends, cousins, colleagues, cousins, friends, and Brown Computer Science students. So I put all the advice together, and that's what you'll find below in the table of contents.

Most people ask me about the career-side of software engineering, and only occasionally about the technical side. Which isn't actually surprising, because university doesn't teach you how to get paid fairly and find high-autonomy work.

The book costs money?

As engineers we aren't inclined to pay for anything, which is why I've made the first half of every chapter free. If you don't buy the book so you can read the 2nd halves, you'll miss out on $1,000s to $10,000s in your first tech industry job alone.

By taking my advice, you'll give yourself an early and sustained salary advantage that accrues to millions over the course of your career. If you ever plan to buy a house in the San Francisco Bay Area, you're going to need my help. Read on!
