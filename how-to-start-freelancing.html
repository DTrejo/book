How to Start Freelancing?

  • Try and find an on-campus job that involves doing engineering work
    • Digital services at the library is a good one
    • The admissions office probably has some engineering work as well because probably do
    • The donations office also probably has work for you, they probably need helping finding and contacting the best donors. Fun fact: evertrue.com is founded by a brown alum. Ever true is also our slogan. The company helps universities with donors.
    • Try and do the previous step first. These jobs will probably be easier to get, and you want to get out of catering prep as soon as possible! It pays poorly and does not complement your resume well. Yes, I can count plates really quickly now. BUT NO ONE CARES.
    • Email all the top node contributors, or @mention them on twitter, or ask in IRC if anyone is hiring remote interns or freelancers. Remember from #2, that you are helping THEM by doing work for them. DO NOT WORK FOR FREE. NO ONE SHOULD WORK FOR FREE. NEVER EVER WORK FOR FREE. Consider also emailing brown alums using the brown alumni directory and/or BRUNET, and ask them if they need help with any side projects. Look for titles like “software engineer” “developer” “architect” “front-end engineer” “back-end engineer” and tech employers.
